## Week 3 - Flappy Tram - Initial Prompt

Build "Flappy Tram" — a Flappy Bird clone
set in Melbourne, Australia.
This is Week 3 of "52 apps in 52 weeks
before I turn 52" by Hey I'm Papa.

Standalone React app, own GitHub repo,
deployable to Vercel.

CONCEPT:
The player controls a Melbourne tram
flying through the city skyline.
Obstacles are tram stops / poles the
tram must fly between.
Pastel illustrated art style throughout.
Fun and charming, not dark or gritty.

CONTROLS:
- Desktop: UP arrow key to flap/rise,
  tram falls with gravity otherwise
- Mobile: Tap anywhere on screen to flap
- Space bar also triggers flap on desktop
- Game starts on first flap/tap

GAME MECHANICS:
- Standard Flappy Bird physics:
  gravity pulls tram down continuously,
  each flap gives upward velocity impulse
- Obstacles: pairs of vertical tram poles/
  stops with a gap between them, scrolling
  left continuously
- Gap size should be fair but challenging
- Score increments each time tram passes
  through a gap
- Collision with poles or ground = game over
- Game over screen shows score, high score,
  and restart button
- High score saved to localStorage

VISUALS - draw everything as SVG or
Canvas elements, no external image files:

Background (parallax layers,
slower layers move slower):
Layer 1 - Sky gradient:
  soft pastel pink to lavender
Layer 2 - Distant Melbourne skyline:
  Eureka Tower, Rialto, Arts Centre spire,
  drawn as simple flat pastel silhouettes
  in soft purple/mauve
Layer 3 - Flinders Street Station:
  iconic facade with clocks and dome,
  flat pastel illustration in warm yellow/
  cream tones, closer and larger
Layer 4 - Ground:
  cobblestone or tram track strip
  in warm grey/tan

The Tram (player character):
- Classic Melbourne W-class tram shape
  seen from the side
- Green and yellow pastel colours
- Slight rotation based on velocity
  (nose up when rising, nose down falling)
- Small animation: wheels spin,
  pantograph bounces

Obstacles:
- Vertical tram stop poles in pastel teal
- Small "STOP" sign on top
- Come in pairs with gap between

UI elements:
- Score displayed top centre in clean
  round font
- High score top right
- Start screen: "Flappy Tram" title,
  "Tap or press Space to start" subtitle,
  pastel illustrated tram bouncing gently

AUDIO (optional, implement if straightforward):
- A soft "ding" sound on flap
  (like tram bell)
- A gentle collision sound on game over
- No background music needed
Use the Web Audio API to generate simple
tones rather than external audio files

DIFFICULTY SCALING:
- Scrolling speed gradually increases
  every 10 points
- Gap size slightly decreases every
  20 points
- Cap difficulty at a reasonable maximum
